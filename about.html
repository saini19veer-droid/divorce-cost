About This Tool

Why We Built DivorceCostBreakdown

Divorce is already hard enough. Figuring out what it's going to cost you shouldn't require a law degree or three phone calls to attorneys just to get a ballpark.

What this tool does

DivorceCostBreakdown is a free, private divorce cost calculator built specifically for US residents. You enter a few details about your situation — which state you're in, whether your divorce is contested or uncontested, whether children and assets are involved — and the calculator gives you a realistic cost estimate based on actual court filing fees, typical attorney hourly rates, and mediation costs for your state.

Nothing is sent to any server. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. We don't collect your data, we don't require an account, and we don't sell anything.

Why I built it

I spend a lot of time in personal finance and legal-advice communities, and one pattern kept showing up: person after person asking the same basic questions — how much a divorce actually costs, whether they need a lawyer, what the difference between contested and uncontested even means. The answers people got back were almost always vague ("it depends") or came from someone trying to get them to call a law firm.

I built DivorceCostBreakdown because I wanted there to be one free place where someone could put in a few real details about their situation and get an actual number — or at least a realistic range — instead of a shrug or a sales pitch. Not to replace a lawyer, but so someone can walk into that first consultation already knowing roughly what to expect and what questions to ask.

The calculator covers all 50 US states, distinguishes between contested and uncontested divorce, factors in whether children or significant assets are involved, and shows a side-by-side comparison so you can see what reaching agreement might save you.

How the estimates are calculated

The cost estimates are built from three components:

  • Court filing fees — based on published state court fee schedules. These are fixed costs you pay regardless of anything else.
  • Attorney fees — based on publicly reported average hourly rate ranges by state/region and typical hours spent on contested vs. uncontested matters.
  • Mediation and additional costs — child custody evaluations, asset appraisals, and other variable costs common in more complex divorces.

The estimates are intentionally shown as ranges rather than single numbers, because actual cost depends heavily on how smoothly negotiations go and how much attorney time is ultimately required. The low end assumes minimal conflict. The high end assumes things get complicated. I review and update the underlying figures periodically, but state fees and typical rates do change, so treat this as a starting estimate rather than a final number.
