Divorce Attorney Fees by State: 2026 Hourly Rate Guide

In California, a divorce attorney might charge $450/hour. In Mississippi, the same type of attorney charges $175. That gap determines almost everything about what your divorce costs.

Before you call a divorce attorney, it helps to know what you are walking into. Attorney hourly rates are the biggest variable in divorce costs — and they vary more by geography than most people expect.

What a retainer is and how it works

Most divorce attorneys require a retainer before they begin work — typically $2,500 to $10,000 — that goes into a trust account. The attorney bills against it as they work. When it is depleted, you replenish it. The retainer is not a flat fee. If your case settles quickly and only $1,200 of a $3,000 retainer is used, you get the $1,800 back.

Ask this before you hire anyone Ask the attorney directly: "What do you typically see cases like mine cost, total, in attorney fees?" A good attorney will give you a range based on your situation. If they will not give you any estimate at all, that is useful information about how they operate.

How experience affects the rate

Attorney rates track experience and specialization as much as geography. A family law partner at a large firm charges more than an associate at the same firm. A solo practitioner with 15 years of family law experience is often a better value than a large-firm associate at twice the rate.

More expensive does not always mean better for your situation. For a straightforward uncontested divorce, you do not need the most expensive attorney in town. For a high-conflict custody case or a divorce involving significant business assets, experience matters more and the rate premium may be worth it.

Flat fee options for simple divorces

Many attorneys now offer flat-fee packages for uncontested divorces, ranging from $1,000-$3,500 depending on state and complexity. The flat fee covers a specific scope of work. Anything outside that scope is billed hourly. If your divorce is simple and likely to stay that way, a flat-fee arrangement gives you cost certainty.
